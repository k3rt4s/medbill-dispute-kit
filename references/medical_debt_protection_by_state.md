# State medical-debt protection summary

Quick-reference summary of state laws limiting how providers, collectors, and credit bureaus may handle medical debt. Use when drafting `letter_initial_dispute.md`, `letter_30day_warning.md`, `letter_credit_report_dispute_fcra.md`, and `complaint_state_doi.md` to pick the right state-specific citation. The kit's per-state law packs at `references/laws_state_<code>.md` carry the verbatim statutory text; this file is the index that helps you pick.

Entries below are listed alphabetically. Each entry shows the four most actionable protections, when they exist in that state:

- **Credit reporting**: whether the state has banned or restricted reporting medical debt to consumer credit bureaus.
- **Interest cap**: whether the state caps post-judgment or pre-judgment interest on medical debt.
- **Charity-care screening**: whether the state requires hospitals to determine financial-assistance eligibility before "extraordinary collection action".
- **Itemized bill on demand**: whether the state requires hospitals to produce an itemized bill within a specific window of a written request.

Statutes change. Verify the current text in `references/laws_state_<code>.md` or on the state's official statutes site before citing in a letter. Last reviewed dates appear at the bottom.

## CA — California

- **Credit reporting**: Hospital Fair Pricing Act (Health & Safety Code § 127400) limits collections against patients earning ≤ 400% FPL; no flat credit-reporting ban.
- **Interest cap**: 5% per year cap on medical-debt judgments (Health & Safety Code § 127425).
- **Charity-care screening**: Hospitals must screen all uninsured and high-medical-cost patients under HSC § 127405; reasonable payment plans required.
- **Itemized bill on demand**: HSC § 1339.585 — itemized bill within 14 working days of written request.

## CO — Colorado

- **Credit reporting**: HB23-1126 — medical debt cannot be reported to consumer credit bureaus; effective August 2023.
- **Interest cap**: 3% per year on medical debt (SB23-093).
- **Charity-care screening**: Hospital Discounted Care Program (HB21-1198) requires income-based discounts for patients ≤ 250% FPL; screening required.
- **Itemized bill on demand**: HSC § 25-3-112 — itemized statement within 30 days of request.

## CT — Connecticut

- **Credit reporting**: SB-3 (2021) — medical debt cannot be reported until 60 days past due, prohibits reporting amounts ≤ $500.
- **Interest cap**: 5% per year on medical-debt judgments (CGS § 19a-509b).
- **Charity-care screening**: CGS § 19a-509b requires hospital financial-assistance policy posting and application access.
- **Itemized bill on demand**: CGS § 20-7f — itemized bill within 30 days.

## IL — Illinois

- **Credit reporting**: Medical Debt Relief Act (HB-2719, 2024) — prohibits credit reporting of medical debt.
- **Interest cap**: 9% per year on medical-debt judgments (Code of Civil Procedure § 2-1303).
- **Charity-care screening**: Hospital Uninsured Patient Discount Act (210 ILCS 89) — hospitals must screen for financial assistance and offer discounts to uninsured patients ≤ 600% FPL urban / 300% FPL rural.
- **Itemized bill on demand**: Fair Patient Billing Act (210 ILCS 88) — itemized bill within 30 days of written request.

## MD — Maryland

- **Credit reporting**: Medical Debt Protection Act (2021) — medical debt cannot be reported until 180 days past due; subsequent legislation has tightened further.
- **Interest cap**: 6% per year on medical-debt judgments.
- **Charity-care screening**: HG § 19-214.2 — hospitals must screen for financial assistance before pursuing collection.
- **Itemized bill on demand**: HG § 19-214 — itemized bill within 10 working days of request.

## MN — Minnesota

- **Credit reporting**: Debt Fairness Act (HF3680, 2024) — prohibits medical debt from being reported to consumer credit bureaus; spousal liability for medical debt restricted.
- **Interest cap**: 4% per year on medical debt (Minn. Stat. § 549.09 amended 2024).
- **Charity-care screening**: Minn. Stat. § 62J.80 / Hospital Agreement under § 144.6985 — hospitals must screen patients ≤ 275% FPL for charity care.
- **Itemized bill on demand**: § 62J.81 — itemized bill on request, no specific window stated but reasonable time expected.

## NJ — New Jersey

- **Credit reporting**: P.L. 2023, c. 174 — prohibits medical debt under $500 from credit reporting and limits broader medical-debt reporting.
- **Interest cap**: General 10% pre-judgment / post-judgment limit applies; no medical-specific cap.
- **Charity-care screening**: P.L. 1992, c. 160 — hospitals must offer charity care to patients ≤ 200% FPL and reduced charges through 300% FPL.
- **Itemized bill on demand**: N.J.A.C. 8:31B-4.34 — itemized bill within 30 days of request.

## NY — New York

- **Credit reporting**: Fair Medical Debt Reporting Act (N.Y. Gen. Bus. Law § 380-t, 2023) — medical debt cannot be reported to consumer credit bureaus.
- **Interest cap**: Public Health Law § 18-c — 2% per year cap on medical-debt judgments.
- **Charity-care screening**: PHL § 2807-k — hospitals must offer financial assistance to patients ≤ 300% FPL; standardized application required.
- **Itemized bill on demand**: PHL § 18 — itemized bill within 30 days of written request.

## NC — North Carolina

- **Credit reporting**: No state-law ban as of last review; relies on federal CFPB rule and voluntary bureau policies.
- **Interest cap**: 8% per year general; no medical-specific cap.
- **Charity-care screening**: HHS Medical Debt Reform plan (2024) — hospitals participating in expanded Medicaid (~99 of the state's hospitals) must implement medical-debt relief plans including charity-care screening; not statewide statute.
- **Itemized bill on demand**: General consumer right under N.C.G.S. § 90-21.20 — itemized statement available on request.

## OH — Ohio

- **Credit reporting**: No statewide ban; relies on federal rule.
- **Interest cap**: Statutory rate (currently 8%); no medical-specific cap.
- **Charity-care screening**: ORC 5168.74 — hospitals must develop and publish financial-assistance policy under state Medicaid rules.
- **Itemized bill on demand**: ORC 5159.05 — itemized bill on request; no statutory window.

## RI — Rhode Island

- **Credit reporting**: 2023 statute — medical debt cannot be reported to consumer credit bureaus.
- **Interest cap**: 9% per year general; medical-debt judgments may receive reduced rates.
- **Charity-care screening**: RIGL § 23-17-12 — hospital free-care program required; eligibility ≤ 200% FPL.
- **Itemized bill on demand**: General consumer right; no specific medical statute.

## TN — Tennessee

- **Credit reporting**: No state-law ban; relies on federal rule and voluntary bureau policies.
- **Interest cap**: General 10% post-judgment under Tenn. Code § 47-14-121; no medical-specific cap.
- **Charity-care screening**: No state mandate; relies on federal § 501(r) for nonprofit hospitals and the hospital's own published Financial Assistance Policy.
- **Itemized bill on demand**: Tenn. Code § 68-11-220 — itemized bill within 30 days of written request from patient; failure to comply gives patient right to refuse payment of the disputed portion until itemization is produced.

## TX — Texas

- **Credit reporting**: No state-law ban; relies on federal rule.
- **Interest cap**: General 5% post-judgment under Texas Finance Code § 304.003; no medical-specific cap.
- **Charity-care screening**: Texas Health & Safety Code § 311.045 — hospitals must maintain a charity care policy and screen patients; thresholds vary.
- **Itemized bill on demand**: Texas H&SC § 311.0025 — itemized bill within 10 days of request.

## VT — Vermont

- **Credit reporting**: Act 76 (2023) — medical debt cannot be reported to consumer credit bureaus.
- **Interest cap**: 12% general; no medical-specific cap.
- **Charity-care screening**: 18 V.S.A. § 9456 — hospitals must publish and follow a financial-assistance policy.
- **Itemized bill on demand**: General consumer-right basis.

## VA — Virginia

- **Credit reporting**: 2022 statute — medical debt cannot be reported to consumer credit bureaus.
- **Interest cap**: Va. Code § 8.01-382 general; no medical-specific cap.
- **Charity-care screening**: Va. Code § 32.1-137.4 — hospitals must offer payment plans and charity care under conditions; not full charity-care mandate.
- **Itemized bill on demand**: Va. Code § 32.1-137.05 — itemized bill within 30 days of written request.

## WA — Washington

- **Credit reporting**: SB 5854 (2022) limits medical-debt collection practices; SB 5380 (2023) restricts credit reporting of medical debt under $1,000 or while in dispute.
- **Interest cap**: Charity Care Act caps interest on hospital debt for low-income patients.
- **Charity-care screening**: RCW 70.170.060 (Charity Care Act, expanded 2022) — hospitals must provide free care to patients ≤ 300% FPL and discounted care to ≤ 400% FPL; screening required.
- **Itemized bill on demand**: RCW 70.41.460 — itemized bill within 30 days of written request.

## Federal protections (apply in every state)

- **Federal CFPB medical-debt rule** (final 2025, effective date subject to litigation/political reversal): prohibits credit reporting of most medical debts. Verify current status before citing.
- **Three-bureau voluntary policy** (effective 2022-2023): no paid medical collections on credit reports; no medical collections under $500; one-year aging window before unpaid medical collections appear.
- **Federal No Surprises Act** (42 U.S.C. § 300gg-111): in-network cost-sharing for emergency, OON ancillary at IN facility, and air ambulance.
- **Federal § 501(r)** (26 U.S.C. § 501(r)): non-profit hospitals must establish Financial Assistance Policy, make reasonable efforts to determine eligibility before extraordinary collection action, and limit charges to FAP-eligible patients to amounts generally billed.
- **FDCPA** (15 U.S.C. § 1692 et seq.): debt collector cannot collect on an unvalidated debt during the 30-day validation window after the initial communication.
- **FCRA** (15 U.S.C. § 1681 et seq.): consumer right to dispute and require reinvestigation of credit-report items; furnisher accuracy obligation under § 1681s-2.

## How the drafter uses this file

When rendering a state-law citation in a letter, the drafter resolves the patient's home state from `HEALTHBILLS_PATIENT_STATE` (and, separately, the state where services were rendered from `BILLER_STATE_OVERRIDES`), then:

1. Loads this file plus `references/laws_state_<code>.md` for both states.
2. Cites the most patient-favorable applicable provision; if both states apply, cite both with attribution.
3. Falls back to federal protections if no state provision applies.

For credit-report dispute letters, the drafter cites the state-specific reporting ban (if available) under Ground D, the voluntary bureau policy under Grounds B/C, and the federal CFPB rule under Ground A.

For initial dispute and 30-day warning letters, the drafter cites the state's itemized-bill statute when one exists, and the state's charity-care-screening statute when the bill is from a non-profit hospital.

For complaint_state_doi.md, the drafter cites the bad-faith / unfair-claims-practice statute from the state DOI portal list at `references/doi_portals.md`.

## What's not in this index

- Statute-of-limitations on medical debt (each state's general SOL applies; medical debt is not separately limited in most states).
- Wage-garnishment exemptions specific to medical debt (general wage-garnishment rules apply with limited exceptions in CO, NY, MA).
- Hospital-lien rules for tort recoveries — see `references/laws_state_<code>.md` for the specific lien act in each state, since the lien rule varies more dramatically than the protections above.
- Surprise-billing state analogues (CA AB-72, NY § 3445, TX SB-1264, etc.) that predate the federal NSA; these still apply in some scenarios but the federal NSA is the primary framework now.
